G-Shock DW5000-C

Casio G-Shock is one of the most respected watch brands in the industry due to its sheer design and durability which are entirely unique to the brand.

The story of the G-Shock starts with Kikuo Ibe. One day he accidentally dropped a pocket watch given to him by his father which consequently broke the entire watch. Due to this accident, he wanted to design a watch that was indestructible. To guide the creation of the project, Ibe came up with the triple 10 resistance rule, which was to represent the 3 factors the watch needed to have: 10-year battery life, 10 bar water resistance, and 10-meter drop survival. To accompany him, Ibe chose 3 individuals to help him design the project and they were known as Team Tough.

Over 200 prototypes for the concept were used and none of them were successful. Ibe’s eureka moment came when he visited a playground and observed children playing with a rubber ball. He realized that an object in the center of a rubber ball wouldn’t sustain any damage from any outside force due to the absorption of force the outside layers of rubber will do. So, he implemented this concept in a prototype and released the first ever G-Shock DW-5000C in 1983.

Sales in Japan were not very high as the Japanese watch market was more focused into dress watches rather than durable watches. To promote G-Shock outside of Japan, Casio released a commercial in America where a hockey player struck a G-Shock like it was a hockey puck to later show that the watch survived the impact of the hit, proving its durability. Although impressive, the commercial was not well-received due to the criticisms of the commercial being unrealistic or misleading. However, an experiment done by a news channel where they hit the watch multiple times with a hockey stick like advertised, showed that the G-Shock was able to sustain such abuse. Because of this experiment, the sales for G-Shock watches rose, gaining the trust of consumers, selling over 19 million units by 1998, and 100 million shipments in 2017.

The watch has been used by the military, paramedics, and law enforcement as tools. The G-Shock has been certified to be used in spaceflight by N.A.S.A. making the G-Shock one of the most worn watches in space. With the ability to change the color of the resin of the watch, the G-Shock has become a fashion icon throughout the years, appearing often in pop culture. To further prove the watch’s durability, Casio took on a challenge no watch manufacturer has taken before. In 2017, Casio attempted the Guinness World Record for the heaviest vehicle to drive over a watch. The G-Shock sustained the weight of the 25-metric ton truck and was able to keep its functions as normal, further proving its reliability and durability.
